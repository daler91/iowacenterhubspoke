02

Design principles

The four rules every screen lives by. Break one, and the platform loses its edge — ambiguity in logistics is fatal.
01 / 04

Clarity is king

In logistics, ambiguity is fatal. Every pixel serves a purpose. If it doesn't communicate state or enable action, it comes out.

02 / 04

Radiate from the hub

Layouts, maps, and diagrams emanate from a central point. Des Moines sits at the center; spokes extend outward. Visual weight follows the metaphor.

03 / 04

Time is physical

Drive time has weight — dashed, gray, textured. Class time has energy — solid, saturated indigo. You can feel the trip.

04 / 04

Iowa-grounded, not rustic

The roots are earthy — soil, corn, sky. The execution is a precise SaaS instrument. No wood textures, no wheat motifs. Flat, high-contrast, anchored.

A central hub, six spokes. No wordmark file exists — build it from the mark + Manrope 800.

The HubSpoke mark is a literal diagram: a filled hub at the center, six outbound connections to smaller spoke nodes. It carries the metaphor in two seconds. The wordmark is Manrope ExtraBold, all-lowercase, with the .scheduler suffix drawn in muted ink for registration.

05

Semantic color tokens

Every state has a soft (background) and strong (fill/text) pair. Never use raw bg-blue-500 — reach for the semantic token.

Inside the app, color is meaning. Indigo always means "hub action." Teal always means "spoke." Ownership — internal vs. partner-run — gets its own blue/purple pair so schedulers can sort by organization at a glance without a legend.

Typography

Manrope for headings and display. Inter for UI and body. JetBrains Mono for data — times, HEX codes, IDs.

Headings get Manrope's slight geometric warmth — it feels built, not generic. Body copy is Inter at 16px with a 1.65 line-height for density that doesn't strain the eye on ops-heavy screens. Every number in the product uses a monospaced, tabular-lined face because shifting digits in a schedule is how mistakes happen.

Schedule blocks

The signature pattern. Class time has energy (indigo, solid). Drive time has weight (gray, dashed diagonal). Town-to-town gets amber.

Every class block in the calendar is automatically bracketed by a drive-time block derived from the distance between the previous location and this one. The visual contrast — solid vs. hatched — lets a scheduler spot an over-packed day in a single glance.

11

Voice & tone

Sentence case. Second person. Times in 12-hour. Talk like a dispatcher briefing a driver: short, specific, next-action oriented.

Do

  • Write like a dispatcher. "Ames class at 9 am. 38 min drive. Leave Des Moines by 8:20."
  • Surface the next decision. Copy should always answer "what do I do next?" — Assign driver, Confirm route, Resolve conflict.
  • Use second person. "Your Wednesday has two town-to-town trips."
  • Name the cities. Never "the spoke location." Always "Marshalltown."
  • Keep numbers in mono. 42h 15m, 08:45, $1,240.
  • Sentence case. Generate weekly report, not Generate Weekly Report.

Don't

  • No corporate fluff. "Empowering your scheduling journey" — cut it.
  • No emoji in UI. Use Lucide icons. ✨📅🚗 are banned in product surfaces.
  • No acronym soup. "ICH uses RBAC w/ JWT" on a user-facing screen is a failure.
  • No Title Case buttons. Sentence case everywhere — proper nouns excepted.
  • No vague error text. Not "Something went wrong." → "Couldn't save: Marshalltown class overlaps with 10:30 drive from Ames."
  • No gradients for meaning. Color is semantic. A gradient button carries no hub/spoke signal.
